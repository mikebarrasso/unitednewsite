### Questions & Answers

**What does "fee-only" mean, and why does it matter?**

Fee-only means we are compensated exclusively by the fees our clients pay us — never by commissions, referral fees, or product sales. This matters because it avoids the conflicts of interest that arise when advisors earn commissions or sell products — common in the financial industry. When an advisor earns a commission for recommending a particular product, it's difficult to know whether that recommendation is truly in your best interest. With a fee-only model, our only incentive is to give you the best advice we can.

---

**How is United Financial Planning Group different from other financial advisors?**

Most advisory firms focus on one piece of the puzzle — usually investment management. If you need tax planning, they refer you out. If you need a tax return prepared, you're on your own.

We built our firm differently. Our team includes Certified Financial Planners, CPAs, and Enrolled Agents working together under one roof. That means your financial plan, your investment strategy, your tax plan, and your tax return are all created and managed by people who talk to each other daily. The result is advice that's more coordinated, more tax-efficient, and more aligned with your actual goals.

---

**What does it mean that you're a fiduciary?**

As a registered investment advisor, we are legally obligated to act in your best interest at all times. This is known as a fiduciary duty. Not all financial professionals are held to this standard — many operate under a lower "suitability" standard, which only requires that recommendations be suitable for you, not necessarily the best option available. We believe the fiduciary standard should be the minimum, not the exception.

---

**Who are your typical clients?**

We work with individuals and families who have accumulated meaningful wealth and want thoughtful, coordinated guidance. Many of our clients are approaching or already in retirement. Others are business owners, corporate executives with equity compensation, startup founders, or high-net-worth individuals navigating complex financial situations. What they share is a desire for one trusted team who sees the full picture.

---

**Do I need to live in New York to work with you?**

No. While we have offices in the New York metro area, we serve clients nationwide. Much of our work is done through video calls and secure digital tools, and our planning and tax preparation process works seamlessly regardless of where you're located.

**How does the planning process work?**

It starts with a conversation. We'll spend time understanding your financial situation, your concerns, and your goals. From there, we develop a comprehensive plan that addresses your investments, retirement timeline, tax situation, and anything else that's relevant. The plan isn't a one-time deliverable — it's an ongoing relationship where we monitor, adjust, and coordinate as your life evolves.

We believe in full transparency around what you pay. Our fees depend on the scope of services and the complexity of your situation. We're happy to walk you through our fee structure in detail during an initial conversation — with no obligation and no pressure.

---

**I already have a financial advisor. Why would I switch?**

You may not need to. But if you've ever felt frustrated that your advisor doesn't understand your tax situation, or that your CPA and advisor seem to give conflicting advice, or that nobody is connecting the dots across your financial life — that's exactly the gap we were built to fill. Many of our clients come to us after years with traditional advisors who were good at one thing but couldn't see the full picture.

**What credentials does your team hold?**

These represent the highest standards in both financial planning and tax expertise.

**Do you help with stock options and equity compensation?**

Yes — this is one of our core strengths. Equity compensation (ISOs, NSOs, RSUs, ESPPs) involves complex tax timing decisions that can significantly impact your net outcome.
